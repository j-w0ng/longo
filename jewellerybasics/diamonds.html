Carat

Carat is an industry standard measurement referring to the weight of the diamond (1 carat equals 0.2g). Aside from the weight, the diamond proportions are important considerations to keep in mind. Proportionate diamonds will produce maximal sparkle, reflecting more light, with the greatest appearance in size.

Clarity

The clarity of the diamond refers to how clear it is. Typically these imperfections are seen using a Loupe which are small magnifying glasses every jeweller uses. Imperfections could be small dots, foggy patches, inclusions, clouds or feathers seen within the diamond. Imperfections are separated into grades which rate the diamond's degree of flawness.

Clarity Grade Description
Flawless - FL No imperfections on the diamond.
Internally Flawless - IF Minor blemishes on the exterior with no inclusions within the jewel.
Very Very Slightly Included - VVS1 & VVS2 Small inclusions, diffcult to see even under Loupe.
Very Slightly Included - VS1 & VS2 Minute inclusions inside of the diamond, and may appear cloudy, but cannot see with naked eye.
Slightly Included - SI1 & SI2 More feathers, clouds and inclusions which may be seen with the naked eye.
Included - I1, I2 & I3 Will see imperfections within the diamond, affecting the overall brilliance of the diamond.

Color

Color is considered to be how yellow a diamond appears. The color scale for diamonds ranges from D to Z, D being the whitest and Z being a faded yellow. Diamonds that are extremely yellow, a type of fancy diamond, are sought after and expensive.

Cut Name How It Looks Like
